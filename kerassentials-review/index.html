Ingredients — Full Analysis

Lavender Oil

Dual mechanism: direct antifungal activity against Trichophyton rubrum (the primary nail fungus) and keratin protection for the nail plate. Published research confirms lavender's antifungal efficacy. The anti-inflammatory properties reduce the tissue irritation around infected nails that worsens with prolonged infection.

Tea Tree Oil (Melaleuca Alternifolia)

The most extensively researched natural antifungal compound available. Multiple clinical trials confirm Tea Tree Oil's activity against T. rubrum, T. mentagrophytes, and Candida species. The terpinen-4-ol component disrupts fungal cell membranes — the mechanism responsible for its antifungal efficacy.

Lemongrass Oil

Citral and geraniol compounds provide additional antifungal activity through cell membrane disruption. Also provides antiseptic protection against the secondary bacterial infections that commonly accompany nail fungus. The combination with Tea Tree Oil creates complementary antifungal mechanisms.

Organic Flaxseed Oil

Rich in alpha-linolenic acid — an omega-3 fatty acid that reduces inflammation at the nail bed and supports the skin barrier around infected nails. The fatty acid profile enhances penetration of active antifungal compounds into the nail plate through lipid-mediated transport.

Aloe Vera

Acemannan compounds provide anti-inflammatory and tissue-healing support at the nail bed and cuticle. Reduces the chronic inflammation that both results from fungal infection and creates conditions favorable for its persistence. Also provides humectant properties that maintain nail plate flexibility during treatment.

Important: Kerassentials is for external use only. Do not apply to open wounds or broken skin. Discontinue if significant skin irritation develops. Tea Tree Oil can cause contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals — test a small area first. Consult your healthcare provider for nail infections accompanied by pain, spreading redness, or swelling, which may indicate bacterial co-infection requiring medical treatment.
