Why the Endothelium Is the Key to Male Sexual Health

The endothelium — the single-cell lining inside blood vessels — produces nitric oxide on demand through endothelial nitric oxide synthase (eNOS). In sexual response, NO release triggers cyclic GMP production, which signals smooth muscle relaxation in the corpus cavernosum, allowing it to fill with blood and maintain the hydraulic pressure required for erection. Age, inflammation, oxidative stress, and metabolic dysfunction all impair eNOS activity — reducing NO output and creating the vascular insufficiency that underlies most cases of ED in men over 40.

Prescription medications work by blocking PDE5 — the enzyme that breaks down cyclic GMP — on demand. ErecPower addresses the upstream problem: restoring endothelial function so that more NO is produced in the first place, combined with gentle natural PDE5 inhibition and testosterone support for comprehensive coverage.

The 4 Pathways ErecPower Targets

Pathway 1

Dual Nitric Oxide Support

L-Citrulline converts to L-Arginine in the kidneys — bypassing the arginase degradation that limits oral arginine bioavailability — raising plasma arginine and providing more substrate for eNOS. Hawthorn Berry's polyphenols protect eNOS from oxidative inactivation, preserving the enzyme that produces NO. Together: more substrate and more enzyme, both available simultaneously.

Pathway 2

Natural PDE5 Inhibition

Epimedium — standardized for icariin — inhibits PDE5, the enzyme that breaks down cyclic GMP. Cyclic GMP is the second messenger that signals smooth muscle relaxation in penile vasculature. This is the same enzyme target as pharmaceutical ED medications, at a milder and more sustained botanical inhibition level.

Pathway 3

Testosterone Support

Tribulus Terrestris stimulates luteinizing hormone from the pituitary → Leydig cell testosterone production. Tongkat Ali (if standardized) reduces sex hormone binding globulin → more free testosterone available. Chrysin inhibits aromatase → less testosterone converting to estrogen. Korean Red Ginseng adds adaptogenic testosterone support through HPA axis modulation.

Pathway 4

Energy & Cortisol Management

Maca Root modulates the HPA axis — reducing cortisol's testosterone-suppressing effect and improving libido through a mechanism independent of direct hormonal activity. Magnesium supports testosterone binding, reduces stress hormone levels, and supports the nervous system signaling involved in sexual arousal. Niacin provides vasodilating cardiovascular support.
