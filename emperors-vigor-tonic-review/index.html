Ingredients — Full Analysis

Dodder Seed (Cuscuta Chinensis)

Used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries for male reproductive vitality. Modern research identifies flavonoid compounds that support testosterone production through LH stimulation and protect Leydig cells from oxidative damage. The traditional use — specific to male reproductive function — predicted the mechanisms modern pharmacology has now validated.

Cnidium Monnieri

The most pharmacologically interesting ingredient in the formula. Osthole — the primary bioactive compound — inhibits PDE5 through the same mechanism as pharmaceutical ED medications, through a natural pathway. Published research confirms nitric oxide stimulation and smooth muscle relaxation. Traditional Chinese medicine classified it specifically as a male vitality compound two millennia before the PDE5 mechanism was described.

Cistanche Tubulosa

Desert plant used in traditional Chinese medicine as a male tonic, called 'ginseng of the desert.' Modern research identifies echinacoside and phenylethanoid glycosides that support testosterone production, reduce oxidative stress in reproductive tissue, and improve energy production at the mitochondrial level.

Rehmannia Root

One of the foundational herbs in traditional Chinese kidney and adrenal support — the traditional system that corresponds closely to what modern endocrinology calls the HPA axis. Published research shows Rehmannia's iridoid glycosides support adrenal function and cortisol regulation, addressing the hormonal environment that determines testosterone availability.

Eucommia Bark

Traditionally used for kidney and liver support — the organ systems traditional Chinese medicine associates with male vitality. Modern research identifies compounds that support testosterone production, improve bone density (which correlates with testosterone levels), and provide antioxidant protection for male reproductive tissue.

Important: Men with hormone-sensitive conditions (prostate cancer, testicular cancer) should not use testosterone-supporting supplements. Men on nitrate medications, PDE5 inhibitors, blood pressure medications, or anticoagulants should consult their physician — Cnidium Monnieri affects vascular tone through PDE5 inhibition. Not for use under 18.
