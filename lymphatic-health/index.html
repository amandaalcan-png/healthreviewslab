Why the Lymphatic System Is Overlooked — and Why It Matters

The lymphatic system is the body's drainage and immune network — a parallel circulation that moves fluid, waste, and immune cells through a vessel system that runs alongside the bloodstream. Unlike the cardiovascular system, it has no dedicated pump. It depends on movement, muscle contractions, and hydration to keep flowing. When it slows — from sedentary work, illness recovery, chronic inflammation, or aging — fluid accumulates in tissues, producing the puffiness, leg heaviness, and circulatory sluggishness that many people accept as inevitable. The supplement category addressing this is smaller and less crowded than metabolic or joint health, which means real mechanistic differentiation is more achievable for products with credible formulations.
