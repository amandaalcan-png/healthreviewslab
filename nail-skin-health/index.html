Key Mechanisms — What the Research Covers

The following mechanism summaries reflect the published literature on ingredients and conditions commonly covered in specialty health supplementation.

Nail Fungus Biofilm Protection

Onychomycosis (nail fungus) reinfection rates exceeding 50% after oral antifungal treatment reflect the biofilm mechanism: fungi form structured communities encased in protective polysaccharide matrices that pharmaceutical compounds struggle to penetrate. Oil-based topical formulas that penetrate the nail plate keratin matrix can deliver antifungal compounds directly to the biofilm — changing the treatment equation fundamentally.

Gut-Skin Axis Inflammation

The gut-skin axis describes how gut microbiome dysbiosis drives systemic inflammation through increased intestinal permeability (leaky gut) — allowing lipopolysaccharides to enter circulation and manifest in skin tissue. Published dermatological research consistently correlates gut microbiome diversity with skin aging outcomes. Oral probiotic formulas targeting gut barrier integrity address this internal driver that topical skincare cannot reach.

AREDS-2 and Macular Degeneration

The Age-Related Eye Disease Study 2 (AREDS-2) is a 20-year NIH-funded randomized controlled trial on 4,000+ participants showing a specific nutrient combination reduces AMD progression risk by 25% over five years. Lutein and Zeaxanthin — the primary active compounds — accumulate in the macular pigment and protect photoreceptors from oxidative damage and blue light exposure.

What is the gut-skin axis?
The gut-skin axis describes the bidirectional relationship between gut microbiome health and skin appearance. What is the AREDS-2 eye health protocol?
AREDS-2 is a 20-year National Eye Institute clinical trial proving a specific nutrient combination reduces AMD progression risk by 25%. The protocol includes Lutein, Zeaxanthin, Vitamins C and E, Zinc, and Copper. iGenics is one of the few consumer supplements formulated to this exact protocol.
Why does nail fungus keep coming back after treatment?
