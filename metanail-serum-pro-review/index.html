Ingredients — Full Analysis

Tea Tree Oil (Melaleuca)

The most clinically validated natural antifungal compound. Terpinen-4-ol disrupts fungal cell membrane integrity through a mechanism distinct from azole antifungals — meaning it remains effective against strains that have developed pharmaceutical resistance. Multiple clinical trials confirm efficacy against the primary nail fungus species.

Hyaluronic Acid

Provides moisture retention in the nail bed — the tissue beneath the nail plate that determines nail health and growth rate. Dehydrated nail beds produce brittle, slow-growing nails that are more susceptible to fungal invasion. Hyaluronic Acid's water-binding capacity maintains the nail bed environment that supports healthy nail production.

Gotu Kola (Centella Asiatica)

Asiaticoside compounds stimulate collagen synthesis in nail bed tissue — directly supporting the structural integrity of new nail growth. Published research on Gotu Kola's wound healing and tissue regeneration effects is substantial. In nail health specifically, it supports the dermal layer from which nail plate cells originate.

Horsetail Extract

Rich in silica — the mineral most critical for nail strength and hardness. Silica is incorporated into keratin cross-links in the nail plate, directly determining how hard and chip-resistant nails are. Horsetail provides the highest bioavailable silica concentration of any botanical source.

Sweet Almond Oil

Provides essential fatty acids for nail plate flexibility — preventing the brittleness that makes nails susceptible to splitting and infection. Also acts as a carrier oil that enhances penetration of the active antifungal and structural compounds into the nail plate through lipid-mediated transport.

Important: For external use only. Do not apply to open wounds, broken skin, or areas with active dermatitis. Tea Tree Oil may cause contact sensitivity in some individuals — patch test before full application. Clove Oil is potent and should not be applied to sensitive or broken skin at full concentration. Consult your healthcare provider for nail infections with surrounding skin breakdown, pain, or signs of bacterial co-infection.
