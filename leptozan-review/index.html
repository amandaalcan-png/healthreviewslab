What Is Leptozan?

Leptozan is a weight management supplement built around leptin resistance — a hormonal signaling failure where the brain stops responding normally to leptin, the hormone that should signal adequate energy stores. It combines BHB ketone salts, a Dead Sea mineral complex, and Berberine to address the problem from three angles: brain, hormone, and metabolic levels.

Why Caloric Restriction Stops Working

Leptin is produced by fat cells in proportion to stored energy, signaling the hypothalamus that energy reserves are adequate. In leptin-resistant individuals, the hypothalamus stops responding to this signal despite elevated leptin levels — perceiving something like starvation regardless of actual body fat. Reducing food intake deepens this perceived starvation signal without addressing the underlying receptor insensitivity, which is why dieting becomes progressively harder rather than easier for many people.

How Leptozan Addresses Leptin Resistance

BHB (beta-hydroxybutyrate) ketone salts are one of the few nutritional compounds identified in research as capable of crossing the blood-brain barrier and directly modulating hypothalamic leptin receptor sensitivity — addressing the central neural component of leptin resistance directly. The Dead Sea mineral complex provides magnesium, calcium, and trace minerals in ratios associated with improved insulin sensitivity, addressing the peripheral metabolic component. Berberine activates AMPK, the cellular energy sensor that modulates glucose disposal and the insulin sensitivity that proper leptin signaling depends on.

Leptozan Ingredients

BHB (Beta-Hydroxybutyrate)

Crosses the blood-brain barrier through the MCT1 transporter, modulating hypothalamic function through AMPK signaling and inflammatory pathways linked to receptor insensitivity.

Dead Sea Mineral Complex

Magnesium, calcium, and trace minerals in ratios associated with improved insulin sensitivity and metabolic conditions supporting leptin responsiveness.

Berberine

Activates AMPK; multiple human clinical trials show effects on body weight, fasting glucose, and insulin resistance comparable in some studies to metformin.
