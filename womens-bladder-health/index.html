Why the Urinary Microbiome Matters for Bladder Control

Bladder urgency, leaks, and frequent nighttime urination affect an estimated 70 million women in the US — and the majority accept these symptoms as inevitable parts of aging. The underlying biology tells a different story. The urinary tract has its own bacterial ecosystem, dominated in healthy women by Lactobacillus strains that maintain the pH environment harmful bacteria can't colonize. After 40, hormonal changes reduce the estrogen that these bacteria depend on. The microbiome shifts, harmful bacteria fill the vacuum, and involuntary bladder muscle contractions become more frequent. This mechanism — urinary microbiome disruption — is different from structural pelvic floor weakness, and it responds to different interventions.
