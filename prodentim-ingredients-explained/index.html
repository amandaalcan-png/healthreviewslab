Why Oral-Specific Strains Matter

Most probiotic supplements are formulated for gut health — their strains are selected for intestinal colonization, survive stomach acid, and colonize the intestinal lining. These strains are not necessarily suited for the oral cavity, which has a different pH, different tissue types, and different pathogen populations than the gut.

ProDentim uses strains specifically selected for oral cavity activity — each with documented research on their behavior in the mouth rather than the gut. The 3.5 billion CFU is also delivered sublingually, directly in the oral cavity, rather than swallowed whole where stomach acid would destroy the strains before they could interact with oral tissue.

The 5 Probiotic Strains — Complete Breakdown

1

Lactobacillus Reuteri

Primary Gum Health Strain

Lactobacillus Reuteri is the most studied strain for gum health specifically. It produces reuterin — a broad-spectrum antimicrobial glycerol-derived compound that inhibits periodontal pathogens including Porphyromonas gingivalis, Treponema denticola, and Fusobacterium nucleatum. Unlike chemical antimicrobials, reuterin is produced selectively in the oral microbiome environment and targets harmful bacteria without disrupting beneficial bacterial populations. Multiple randomized controlled trials on L. Reuteri lozenges show statistically significant reduction in gingival bleeding index and plaque scores after 3-4 weeks.

Reuterin Production Periodontal Pathogen Inhibition Gum Bleeding Reduction
2

Lactobacillus Paracasei

Competitive Exclusion + Breath Freshness

Lactobacillus Paracasei is a colonization specialist — it has strong affinity for oral mucosal surfaces and tooth enamel adhesion sites, making it effective at competitive exclusion of harmful bacteria including Streptococcus mutans. When L. Paracasei occupies adhesion sites first, S. mutans and other cariogenic bacteria have fewer attachment points — reducing plaque formation at the source. L. Paracasei is also studied for its role in reducing volatile sulfur compound (VSC) production, which is the primary cause of halitosis (bad breath).

Competitive Exclusion S. mutans Inhibition Breath Freshness
3

B.lactis BL-04® (Patented)

Immune Modulation

B.lactis BL-04 is a patented strain from IFF (International Flavors & Fragrances) with the strongest immune-modulating evidence of any strain in the formula. Its primary mechanism in oral health is supporting secretory IgA (sIgA) production in oral mucosal tissue — sIgA is the dominant local immune defense in the oral cavity, neutralizing pathogens at mucosal surfaces before they can establish infection. B.lactis BL-04 also reduces the systemic inflammatory load that reaches oral tissue, making it the formula's anti-inflammatory agent at the immune level rather than the microbial level.

Secretory IgA Stimulation Mucosal Immune Support Anti-Inflammatory
4

Lactobacillus Salivarius

Natural Resident + Hydrogen Peroxide Production

Lactobacillus Salivarius is a natural resident of healthy oral cavities — it has high affinity for oral mucosal surfaces and produces hydrogen peroxide as a metabolic byproduct. Low-level hydrogen peroxide production inhibits catalase-negative oral pathogens without damaging enamel at the concentrations produced by bacterial metabolism. L. Salivarius also produces bacteriocins — protein-based antimicrobial compounds — with activity against Streptococcus pyogenes and other streptococcal pathogens that colonize the oral cavity and throat.

H₂O₂ Antimicrobial Production Bacteriocin Production Natural Oral Resident
5

Streptococcus Salivarius

BLIS Production — Broad Pathogen Suppression

Streptococcus Salivarius is the dominant beneficial bacterium in healthy human oral microbiomes — it colonizes the tongue and oral mucosa within hours of birth and remains the primary oral commensal throughout life. S. Salivarius produces bacteriocin-like inhibitory substances (BLIS) — specifically BLIS K12 and M18 strains — that suppress a wide range of periodontal pathogens. BLIS K12 has documented activity against Streptococcus pyogenes, Fusobacterium nucleatum, and Prevotella intermedia. It is the closest to a "master regulator" of the healthy oral microbiome ecosystem.

BLIS K12 Production Broad-Spectrum Pathogen Suppression Dominant Oral Commensal

3.5 Billion CFU — Why This Number?

Sublingual delivery means the bacteria are released directly in the oral cavity — not swallowed through stomach acid. 3.5 billion CFU via melt-in-mouth tablet delivers a higher effective dose to oral tissues than 50 billion CFU swallowed in a capsule, where most bacteria are destroyed before reaching the mouth at all. The CFU number should always be evaluated in context of delivery format.

Supporting (Non-Probiotic) Ingredients

Inulin

Prebiotic fiber that specifically feeds Bifidobacteria and Lactobacillus strains. Provides the nutritional substrate the probiotic strains need to colonize and establish populations in the oral cavity.

Malic Acid

Naturally found in apples — supports tooth whitening by helping remove surface stains. Also maintains oral pH in the range where beneficial bacteria thrive and enamel mineralization is favored.

Tricalcium Phosphate

Provides calcium and phosphate ions that support enamel remineralization. Enamel naturally loses minerals through acid exposure — tricalcium phosphate restores the mineral substrate.

Peppermint

Provides menthol for breath freshness and has mild antimicrobial activity against several oral pathogens. Also contributes to the palatability of the melt-in-mouth format, supporting consistent daily use.
