The Problem With Conventional Oral Care

Conventional oral hygiene operates on an elimination model — toothpaste, mouthwash, and antibacterial rinses are designed to kill bacteria broadly. This approach addresses harmful bacteria effectively in the short term but has a significant unintended consequence: it simultaneously eliminates the beneficial bacterial populations that are the oral cavity's primary natural defense system.

Research published in Springer Nature found that people with consistently good dental health — low rates of cavities, gum disease, and oral inflammation — have measurably higher populations of beneficial oral bacteria. The correlation is directional: beneficial bacteria aren't just a byproduct of good oral health; they appear to be a contributing cause of it.

The 4 Core Mechanisms Behind ProDentim

1. Competitive Exclusion

Beneficial bacteria introduced through ProDentim compete with harmful bacteria for adhesion sites on tooth enamel and gum tissue. Bacteria must physically attach to oral surfaces to colonize and cause damage. When beneficial strains occupy these sites first, harmful bacteria have fewer attachment points — reducing plaque formation at the source.

2. Antimicrobial Compound Production

Lactobacillus Reuteri specifically produces reuterin — a broad-spectrum antimicrobial compound with documented activity against periodontal pathogens. Unlike mouthwash, reuterin is produced selectively in the oral environment and targets harmful bacteria without eliminating beneficial populations.

3. pH Regulation

Harmful oral bacteria — particularly Streptococcus mutans — produce lactic acid as a metabolic byproduct, lowering oral pH below the threshold where enamel begins to demineralize (pH 5.5). Beneficial bacterial populations help buffer oral pH, reducing the acid exposure time that drives enamel erosion.

4. Immune Modulation

B.lactis BL-04® has documented effects on mucosal immune function — supporting the oral cavity's secretory IgA production, which is the primary local immune defense that neutralizes pathogens at mucosal surfaces before they can establish infection.

The 5 Bacterial Strains — What Each Does

Streptococcus Salivarius
Dominant beneficial bacterium in healthy oral microbiomes. Produces bacteriocin-like inhibitory substances (BLIS) that suppress periodontal pathogens.

Why a Chewable Tablet — Not a Capsule

Delivery format matters for oral probiotics in a way that differs from gut probiotics. Capsules are swallowed and dissolve in the stomach — the bacteria reach the gut but have minimal contact with the oral cavity. For oral health benefits, the bacteria need to be released in the mouth, where they can begin competing for adhesion sites on tooth enamel and gum tissue immediately. Chewable tablets that dissolve slowly in the mouth maximize contact time between the probiotic strains and the oral surfaces they need to colonize.
